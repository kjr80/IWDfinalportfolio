Good Design

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

We always have to think about who is going to be seeing our design and how that is going to make them feel. This is especially apparent in our UX Testing Logo Project. As designers, we need to make sure we are getting the correct message across to whoever is viewing our designs.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Design principles are incredibly important to take into consideration when making new design. We were able to try putting some to use in our Logo A/B/C project, and we were able to analyze them in our Good Design project. The principles, such as color, balance, repetition, etc, all lend themselves to making a design "good" when used correctly. It's important for a designer to be able to recognize them and incorporate them into their own designs.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

For each project, we have been able to workshop ideas off of one another. We show each other our designs and talk about what worked for us and what we had trouble with. Others then tell us what they think of the design, and different tips for fixing our struggles.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

The very process of making this portfolio allows us to practice revision. We are able to do these different projects, and then talk about them with others in class. We can bounce ideas off of each other to change our designs. Then we can implement those ideas and suggestions when it comes time to submit them all again as a portfolio.

5. Learn to present your work.

The UX Testing Logo Project has especially given us some practice with presenting our work. This was a more complex project, which means there was much more to share. We were able to figure out an efficient way to communicate our findings in a presentation.

Short Form


Two-Page Spread

Graphic Design Resume

Advertisement

Client Project

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

This topic has a big emphasis on what the user will see and think of your design. This is something we had to think about especially in this batch of projects. We need to make sure we're getting the correct message across in things like resumes and advertisements. Especially in the client project, we needed to make sure our client's message was correctly conveyed.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Design principles such as color, balance, and alignment all had to be taken heavily into account with these projects. Especially in the advertisement, you can't have bad alignment or color choice because the user will never look at the ad long enough to decide to buy the product. These principles are also the main factors that we could change across different samples for our client project. Playing around with them and deciding which ones to change and which to keep the same kept us moving towards a final design.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

We were able to work with our peers by showing our current designs after each project was started. We do this in small groups first and then in front of the whole class. This lets us get a general consensus of what the user could possibly like or have issues with, and then we can adjust the design accordingly. Our classmates also have some ideas that we hadn't thought of or considered, so we can take those suggestions into account when revising.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

While revision happens after workshopping each of the projects, it was especially used in our Client Project. We had to come up with a first stage of designs, then change them based on what the client wanted. We talked with our client about design choices such as font, layout, and image choices. The client would see their options and decide which choices met their goal. This cycle of revision eventually led to the final design.

5. Learn to present your work.

We presented our work to our fellow classmates. We had to talk about things we enjoyed or struggled with. And sometimes after taking some constructive criticism, we have to explain our reasoning behind making certain design decisions. We only get a small amount of time to take our turn sharing, so we have to come up with an efficient way to present our projects.

UX Design Branding Project

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

Once again, we as designers have had to put ourselves in the shoes of the people who will see our designs. We need to predict how they will perceive the design to make sure it aligns with our goals. This was extra evident in our UX Design Branding Project, where we had to come up with a mission statement and values of the brand, and make sure those came across in the logo we designed.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

In all three of our last projects, we had to pay close attention again to how we used our different design principles. The main ones I made sure to fully focus on were color and alignment/space. This is because we had a lot more discussion on accessibility during this time. With our color choices, we have to consider that there are people with different types of colorblindness, and our color combinations can cause unintended problems. Alignment/space can also cause problems for those that have issues with sight.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

We discussed our designs with small groups for each project. By showing off what we had and explaining our design decisions to each other, we all were able to talk about the perspective of the designer as well as the viewer. This also created an environment where we felt able to ask questions, and the other students could give their suggestions on way for us to improve our designs.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Revision is an integral part of the design process. As we continued to get feedback from our instructor and our peers, we iterated through different designs until getting to the final one. Revision had the biggest effect in our UX Design Branding project. We had to come up with two sample logo designs, then take feedback from our peers on them. We then had to use that feedback to revise our designs, sometimes more than once, until we got to the optimal design for the viewer.

5. Learn to present your work.

We always get to practice presenting our work in a smaller setting as we peer review. But for the UX Design branding project, we presented in a more professional way. Not only could we figure out the correct language to present with, we also got to be decisive on what we included in our slide presentation. This experience gave us practice with creating slide decks, and figuring what information was pertinent enough to include.
